# Team: Hammiyala Collective

## Activity
- Arabica Coffee – Block Management: Completed machine weeding across all 6 Arabica blocks covering 44 acres.
- Land Survey & Boundary Protection: Conducted individual Survey Number (Sy. No.) survey and demarcation for the entire collective area. Installed boundary fencing post-survey to safeguard against encroachment and litigation. Erected boundary poles in remaining stretches and completed GPS marking for all survey numbers.
- Cardamom – Sprinkler Irrigation: Implemented sprinkler irrigation across all cardamom blocks to maintain optimal soil moisture during dry periods and facilitate pre-monsoon input application.
- Cardamom – Nutrient Application: Applied bio-input NPK in Block 4 to enhance root development and promote pseudostem growth in young cardamom plants.
- Cardamom – Pest Management: Detected shoot borer larval infestation through field inspection; applied Bacillus thuringiensis (BT) as a foliar spray to control the pest at larval stage.
- Water Infrastructure & Equipment: Repaired the 15-year-old 8 HP motor for stream water pumping. Installed a movable trolley to facilitate stream-based water pumping. Covered the top water tank with green shade net to prevent debris entry and reduce sunlight exposure on tank walls.
- Road Work – JCB & Material Shifting: Commenced concrete road construction via JCB from the guest house to the top cluster area (~1.2 km). Material shifting for road work also initiated.
- Pond Expansion: Expanded the pond to approximately 2,600 sq ft with an estimated water holding capacity of 200,000 litres.

## Output
- 6 Arabica blocks (44 acres) fully machine-weeded.
- Complete Sy. No. survey, GPS marking, boundary fencing and pole installation done across the entire collective.
- Sprinkler irrigation operational across all cardamom blocks.
- Bio-input NPK applied in Block 4.
- BT foliar spray applied to address shoot borer infestation.
- 8 HP motor repaired and movable water pumping trolley installed.
- Top water tank covered with green shade net.
- 1.2 km concrete road work commenced; material shifting underway.
- Pond expanded to 2,600 sq ft / 2,00,000 litre capacity.

## Outcome
- Arabica coffee blocks are clean and weed-free, supporting healthy plant growth going into the next crop cycle.
- Land survey and boundary fencing provides legal clarity and protects the collective from encroachment risks.
- Cardamom crop is well-irrigated, nourished, and pest-managed ahead of the monsoon season.
- Water infrastructure is more reliable and better protected — improving irrigation capacity across the collective.
- Road construction will significantly improve internal connectivity between the guest house and upper cluster.
- Expanded pond enhances on-site water storage and supports long-term water security.

## Impact
- Comprehensive crop and land management across Hammiyala strengthens both productivity and legal ownership clarity.
- Pre-monsoon preparation (irrigation, nutrient application, pest control) positions the cardamom crop for a stronger yield.
- Infrastructure investments in roads, water systems, and pond expansion directly enhance the operational and ecological resilience of the collective.
