# Team: CD&S (Collective Design & Science)

## Activity
- Hammiyala Transformation Story (GIS Webpage): Completed and published an interactive, GIS-led narrative webpage for the Hammiyala Collective — "A Landscape in Transformation" — authored by Siri Nallaparaju (published February 12, 2026). The webpage integrates place, ecology, planning, and process through clear spatial zoning (wilderness, farming, community, water systems), linked to real data and biodiversity observations. Handover of the document and orientation call completed with concerned teams.
- Bhopal GIS Story (Work in Progress): Developing a GIS story walkthrough for prospects for the Bhopal Collective — currently in draft stage.
- Farm Staff Training — Microbial Bio-inputs: Conducted hands-on training for farm staff on preparation, dilution protocols, and field application of microbial concentrates to standardise practice and improve soil-plant microbial integration.
- On-Farm Microbial Bio-input Production & Application: Initiated on-farm production of microbial bio-inputs. Carried out staged applications of microbial nutrition (NPK-Zn) and microbial pesticides across Poomaale 1.0 and 2.0 cardamom patches, scaling up to multiple concentrate-dilution batches of 800 litres.
- Pest & Disease Identification Resources: Developed user-friendly pest and disease identification posters and reference materials covering Azhukal/Capsule Rot, Rhizome Rot, and Chenthal/Leaf Blight, alongside a Nutrition Deficiency and Excess reference chart for farm teams.
- Pre-Monsoon Biodiversity Survey Planning: Initiated volunteer sourcing to conduct pre-monsoon biodiversity surveys across Coorg, Mumbai, Bhopal, and Hyderabad collectives. Created a survey calendar with scheduled timelines — Coorg (March–April), Mumbai (May 1st half), Bhopal (May 2nd half), and Hyderabad (June 1st half), with 4–5 volunteers per collective.
- Work in Progress: Setting up Epicollect as a standardised data collection tool; sorting and processing Hammiyala biodiversity survey data from 2025.

## Output
- 1 interactive GIS landscape narrative published for Hammiyala Collective (live webpage).
- 1 Bhopal GIS story in draft/development.
- 1 farm staff training session completed on microbial bio-input preparation and application.
- 800 L of microbial bio-inputs produced and applied across Poomaale 1.0 and 2.0 cardamom patches.
- Pest/disease identification posters and nutrition deficiency reference charts developed and distributed.
- Pre-monsoon biodiversity survey calendar created covering 4 collectives.
- Volunteer recruitment initiated for biodiversity field surveys at Hyderabad and Bhopal collectives.
- Epicollect data tool setup and Hammiyala 2025 biodiversity data sorting in progress.

## Outcome
- Prospects and team members now have an immersive, data-grounded way to understand the Hammiyala landscape — reducing information gaps and aligning internal communication around a shared spatial story.
- Farm staff are equipped with standardised protocols for microbial input preparation, improving consistency and quality of soil-plant treatments across collectives.
- Cardamom crops at Poomaale 1.0 and 2.0 have received scientifically calibrated microbial nutrition and pest management at scale.
- Farm teams have accessible, visual reference materials for identifying and responding to crop diseases and nutritional deficiencies.
- Pre-monsoon biodiversity surveys are planned, resourced, and ready to execute across all 4 collectives in Q2 2026.

## Impact
- The Hammiyala GIS story strengthens prospect engagement and membership conversion by presenting the collective as a living, evolving landscape — not just a land parcel.
- Standardised microbial bio-input training and on-farm production builds long-term agronomic self-reliance across Beforest's collective network.
- Pest and nutrition identification resources reduce crop loss risk and empower field teams to act faster and more accurately.
- The multi-collective biodiversity survey program directly supports Beforest's science-backed conservation narrative and generates data for ecological monitoring, reporting, and member communication.
