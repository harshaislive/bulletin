# Team: Bodakonda Collective (Hyderabad)

## Activity
- Paddy – Transplantation & Soil Enrichment: Completed paddy transplantation and spread vermi compost across paddy fields to enhance soil fertility and support healthy crop establishment.
- Vegetable Harvesting: Harvested a total of 880 kg of vegetables in February, including green beans, brinjal, potatoes, and other produce.
- CZ Drip Line Setup: Carried out trench digging for pipeline installation to enable drip irrigation for CZ plants — 800 metres completed to date.
- Apiculture Flora Plantation: Procured 9 varieties of flowering plants — Plumbago, Stachys, Jatropha, Rotheca serrata, Buddleja asiatica, Star Jasmine, Buddleja davidii (Butterfly Bush), Ixora Yellow, and Vetiver — to establish a sustained bee forage resource. Pit digging and compost filling completed on berms for plantation.
- FPZ Fence Repairing: Repaired fencing in the Farm Protection Zone (FPZ) to secure the agricultural area.
- Kitchen Grey Water Treatment: Implemented grey water treatment system for kitchen wastewater.
- Thermal Compost Preparation: Prepared thermal compost to support soil health across the collective.
- Pigeon Pea Winnowing & Cleaning: Post-harvest processing of pigeon pea carried out through winnowing and cleaning.
- Bio-inputs Application: Applied Jeevamurutham slurry in C1 terrace for papaya plants; applied BVM (Beejamrutha/Bio-Vita Mixture) in C2 terrace.
- Mulching: Applied paddy straw mulching across C4 and C8 terraces for moisture retention and weed control.
- Drip Irrigation Installation: Installed drip irrigation pipeline in C4 and C8 terraces.
- Sowing & Transplantation: Transplanted chilli in C4 terrace; sowed vegetable seeds in C8 terrace.
- Soil Filling: Carried out soil filling for onion and potato plants.
- Weeding: Completed weeding in A3 (pomegranate) and D1 (guava) zones.
- Volunteering Program: Conducted one-day volunteering programs over two consecutive weeks. Activities included Jeevamurutham slurry spreading, harvesting, and flora plantation.
- Rainfall Recorded: 1.34 inches of rainfall received during February.

## Output
- Paddy transplantation completed; vermi compost spread across paddy fields.
- 880 kg of vegetables harvested in February.
- 800 metres of trench dug for CZ drip line pipeline.
- 9 species of apiculture flora procured; pit digging and compost filling done on berms.
- FPZ fencing repaired; grey water treatment system implemented; thermal compost prepared.
- Pigeon pea winnowed and cleaned post-harvest.
- Bio-inputs (Jeevamurutham + BVM) applied across C1 and C2 terraces.
- Paddy straw mulching completed in C4 and C8 terraces.
- Drip irrigation pipelines installed in C4 and C8 terraces.
- Chilli transplanted in C4; vegetable seeds sown in C8.
- Soil filling done for onion and potato plots; weeding completed in A3 and D1 zones.
- 2 volunteering sessions conducted over 2 weeks.
- 1.34 inches of rainfall recorded.

## Outcome
- Paddy crop is well-established with soil nutrition support from vermi compost, setting up a healthy growing cycle.
- Strong vegetable harvest of 880 kg reflects productive farm management across terrace zones.
- CZ drip line and terrace-level drip irrigation installations improve water delivery efficiency and reduce irrigation effort.
- Apiculture flora plantation establishes year-round nectar and pollen availability, directly supporting honeybee colony health and honey production.
- Bio-inputs, mulching, and soil filling across terraces improve soil structure, moisture retention, and plant nutrition holistically.
- Volunteering programs deepened participant engagement with land and farming practices at the collective.
- Rainfall of 1.34 inches provided supplementary natural moisture to crops and plantations.

## Impact
- Comprehensive terrace-level farming activity (sowing, transplantation, drip setup, soil care) positions Bodakonda for strong multi-crop output in the coming months.
- The Apiculture Flora Plantation initiative directly strengthens the beekeeping vertical, contributing to biodiversity and potential honey produce revenue.
- Volunteering programs build community participation and reinforce Beforest's member engagement model at the Hyderabad Collective.
- Sustainable practices across waste (grey water treatment), composting, and bio-inputs reflect a mature, ecology-first operational approach.
