# Team: Bhopal Collective

## Activity

- Installed five wooden plank bridges along the nature trail to ensure safe crossing over nalas (small streams), addressing unsafe gaps especially during rainy seasons.
- Carried out agricultural operations across 10 acres: harvesting Pigeon Pea (4 acres), irrigating and harvesting Mustard (2 acres), and irrigating two Wheat varieties — Sharbati (3 acres) and Khapali (1 acre).
- Applied manure and Jeevamrit across fields for soil enrichment.
- Hosted the first-ever Forest Ranger Experience Event with 12 participants, including a nature walk, vegetable harvesting, biodiversity board creation, tiger pugmark hunting and casting, and a sundowner at the plateau.

## Output

- 5 wooden bridges installed and functional on the nature trail.
- 3 active crops under management (Pigeon Pea, Mustard, Wheat) across 10 acres.
- 1 pilot experience event completed with 12 participants.
- Soil enrichment applied across agricultural plots.

## Outcome

- The nature trail is now fully accessible and safe for visitors year-round, with reduced risk of soil erosion at crossing points.
- Agricultural productivity is supported through timely irrigation, harvesting, and soil health inputs.
- The Forest Ranger Experience pilot provided the team with direct learnings to improve and refine future events.

## Impact
- Improved infrastructure on the nature trail enhances the overall visitor experience and long-term sustainability of the collective.
- Active crop management across 10 acres contributes to operational self-sufficiency and land productivity.
- The successful pilot event establishes a replicable experience program, opening a new engagement channel for participants and potential future revenue.
